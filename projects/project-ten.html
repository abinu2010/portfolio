Boat In A Bottle

Do not touch the glass. A 48-hour Small Boats jam prototype about placing boat parts inside a floating bottle without breaking it.

Category: Game Jams and Experiments | Role: Main Developer | View Repo

About the Jam

Boat In A Bottle was built for the Personal Development Week Jam, a 48-hour jam based on the theme Small Boats. The game became a 2D ship-in-a-bottle prototype where the player places boat parts inside a floating glass bottle without touching the edges.

The fail condition is that touching the bottle breaks it and restarts the game. The success condition is completing the boat inside the bottle, after which the boat transforms into a larger version. The original three-level plan was reduced to one complete challenge to keep the scope achievable.

My Contribution

I worked as the main developer while my teammates focused on art assets. My goal was to build a clear start-to-finish loop: menu, gameplay, win state, lose state, scene transitions, audio, and restart flow.

I developed the bottle wave motion and drift system so the bottle felt like it was floating on water. I then connected the placement mechanic to the win and lose conditions so the player had one readable goal and one immediate failure state.

Technical Notes

  • Wave motion and bottle drift logic to support the floating feel.
  • Bottle setup adjusted so it visually sat inside the water.
  • Win and lose conditions connected to boat-piece placement.
  • Main menu, play flow, quit flow, scene transitions, and audio manager.
  • CapsuleCollider2D was replaced with PolygonCollider2D to better match the bottle opening.
  • Water sprite issue fixed after only the wave crest appeared.
  • Wave presentation changed to a line-rendered crest for clearer water motion.
  • Main lesson: finish the full game loop before over-polishing one mechanic.
